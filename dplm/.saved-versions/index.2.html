Distinguishable-particle lattice model (DPLM) of glass

The following 2 videos show the dynamics of DPLM from kinetic Monte Carlo simulations at a high temperature (T = 0.5) and a low temperature (T = 0.16) respectively. Each system consists of 1592 particles and 8 voids on a 40 x 40 square lattice, i.e. a void density φv = 0.005. Particles are randomly colored to indicate their distinguishable nature. For T = 0.5, voids diffuse independently without trapping. For T=0.16, isolated voids are trapped by the self-generated disorders. Two coupled voids on the top right corner move substantially more vigorously demonstrating dynamic facilitation. (See "Emergent facilitation behaviors in a distinguishable-particle lattice model of glass", Ling-Han Zhang and Chi-Hang Lam.)
